Chicken Enchiladas

Description

Chicken enchiladas with sour cream sauce are a delightful and hearty Mexican-inspired dish. Tender shredded chicken is seasoned with a blend of spices and wrapped in soft corn tortillas. These filled tortillas are then placed in a baking dish, generously smothered in a rich and creamy sour cream sauce infused with green chilies and garlic for a subtle kick. Topped with a layer of melted cheese, the enchiladas are baked until bubbly and golden. The result is a comforting meal with a perfect balance of savory, tangy, and slightly spicy flavors, garnished with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime for added brightness.

Ingredients

  • 1 (16 ounce) container fat-free sour cream
  • 1 (10.5 ounce) can condensed reduced-fat cream of chicken soup
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro
  • cooking spray (such as Pam)
  • 1 (10 ounce) can diced tomatoes with green chile peppers (such as RO*TEL)
  • 1 cup shredded cooked chicken
  • 1 (4 ounce) can diced green chiles
  • 8 (8 inch) flour tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded Colby-pepperjack cheese, divided

Steps

  1. Mix sour cream, condensed soup, and cilantro in a saucepan over medium heat; cook, stirring occasionally, until heated through, 3 to 5 minutes. Remove from the heat.
  2. Spray a large skillet with cooking spray; add diced tomatoes, chicken, onion, and green chiles. Cook and stir over medium heat until onion is transparent, 5 to 10 minutes.
  3. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Spray an 8x11-inch baking dish with cooking spray.
  4. Spoon 2 to 3 tablespoons chicken mixture down the center of each warm tortilla, then sprinkle with about 1 tablespoon pepper Jack cheese. Roll each tortilla around filling and place seam-side down into the prepared baking dish. Pour sour cream sauce over tortillas and sprinkle remaining cheese over top.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven until sauce is bubbling and cheese is melted, 25 to 30 minutes.
